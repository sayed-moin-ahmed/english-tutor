# Focus Areas — phrase banks & before/after rewrites

A learner studies English *for* something. Ask early, store it in `progress/`, and bias examples and corrections toward their focus. Pull from the matching section; don't dump all of them.

---

## Professional / Work

Clear, polite, skimmable. Bias toward concision and the right level of formality.

**Phrase bank**
- Asking: "Could you clarify…?", "Would it be possible to…?", "Just to confirm, you'd like…?"
- Softening: "I think we might want to…", "One option could be…", "Have we considered…?"
- Updating: "Quick update:", "Heads-up:", "For visibility,…"
- Disagreeing politely: "I see it slightly differently —", "I'm not sure that fully covers…"

**Before → after**
- "Send me the file." → "Could you send me the file when you get a chance?"
- "Your code is wrong." → "I think there may be an edge case here — could we double-check?"

### Engineering sub-bank
- **Standup:** "Yesterday I finished X. Today I'm working on Y. I'm blocked on Z."
- **PR description:** imperative + why — "Add retry logic to the cache client to handle transient timeouts."
- **Code review:** "Could we extract this into a helper?" (not "this is messy"). "What happens if `x` is null here?"
- **Design doc:** state the problem, options with trade-offs, the decision, and why. Avoid vague "it should be fast" → "p99 latency under 200ms."

---

## Everyday / General

Natural, relaxed, idiomatic. Contractions and common phrasal verbs are good here.

**Phrase bank**
- Reacting: "No way!", "That makes sense.", "Fair enough.", "Same here."
- Small talk: "How's it going?", "What have you been up to?", "Long time no see."
- Phrasal verbs: *figure out, run into, end up, look forward to, deal with, catch up*.

**Before → after**
- "I am very tired today." → "I'm exhausted today." / "I'm wiped out."
- "It is raining strongly." → "It's pouring."

---

## Academic / Exam (IELTS / TOEFL)

Formal register, hedging, clear structure, cohesive devices.

**Phrase bank**
- Structuring: "Firstly,… Moreover,… In contrast,… Consequently,… To conclude,…"
- Hedging: "It could be argued that…", "This suggests that…", "tends to", "is likely to".
- Citing/comparing: "Whereas X…, Y…", "A significant proportion of…".

**Exam tips**
- IELTS Writing Task 2: intro (paraphrase + thesis) → 2 body paragraphs (point + example) → conclusion.
- Speaking: extend every answer with a reason and an example; don't answer in one word.
- Avoid contractions and slang in formal writing; vary sentence length and linkers.

---

## Travel / Survival

High-frequency, practical, fast.

**Phrase bank**
- "Excuse me, where is…?", "How much is this?", "Could I have…, please?", "Do you accept cards?", "I'd like to book…", "Is there a… nearby?", "Could you say that again, more slowly?"
- Politeness defaults: *please, thank you, sorry, excuse me* — used more often than many learners expect.
